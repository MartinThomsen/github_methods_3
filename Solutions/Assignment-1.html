Exercise 1 - describing the dataset and making some initial plots

  1. Describe the dataset, such that someone who happened upon this dataset could understand the variables and what they contain

A study was conducted to ascertain whether Korean native speakers change their pitch, as a result of speaking in formal or informal scenarios. Seven different scenarios were acted out, where each participant responded to both a formal and informal setup. 16 participants (9 females and 7 males) completed the scenarios and were scored on a number of variables.

There are seven variables contained within the dataset: Subject: The identifier of each participant, where ‘F’ indicates a female participant and ‘M’ a male participant. Gender: The gender of the participant (female or male). This is a factor with two levels and is therefore transformed:

politeness$gender<- factor(politeness$gender)

Scenario: Each participant has completed seven scenarios, which require the participant to speak in a formal or informal manner. Scenarios are given a integer (1-7) as an identifier. Attitude: Attitude denotes whether or not the participant is speaking in a formal or informal manner. This is a factor and must therefore be transformed:

politeness$attitude<- factor(politeness$attitude)

Total duration: Time taken for each participant to complete their interaction. f0mn: Base frequency of the pitch of each participant (mean of pitch in sentence; Hz). hiss_count: Number of time each participant takes an audible breath. `
